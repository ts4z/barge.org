--- date: 2000-04-01 summary: ATLARGE 2000 was held at the Tropicana in Atlantic City. tags: - atlarge - 2000 title: ATLARGE 2000 type: sequential-section --- ATLARGE 2000 was held at the Tropicana in Atlantic City from March 23 through March 26, 2000. The fifth annual gathering was organized by Tiger and Foldem, who had taken over the reins from Jazbo. Thursday evening's smoker dinner at The Old Waterway Inn drew about a dozen attendees, and a Friday morning golf outing at Brigantine Golf Links brought out 12–15 players. Eric "Jaeger" Holtman won the No-Limit Hold'em tournament, famously eliminating Doug Anderson on just the second hand when running spades turned his aces into a flush. Ralph Ballario won the Seven Card Stud event, defeating Jaeger heads-up to take the championship. Ross Poppel won the MATS side event — a no-limit hold'em tourney run in Jerry's hotel room — earning a seat to the Tropicana's TARGET tournament. The Saturday night banquet saw three new ADB inductees: Nanette "No No" Merkelis, John "Luke" Luckini, and Arthur "Arti" Santella, the most inducted at a single ARG event to that point. The baby pot-limit game provided its usual late-night drama, highlighted by Sippy catching the case ten to beat Bill Alan's nut full house. The Tropicana also honored Will Espin with a commemorative $5 chip during the weekend.
