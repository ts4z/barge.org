What is "ATLARGE"?

ATLARGE is an annual gathering in Atlantic City of poker players who originally "met" and/or still participate on the Internet Newsgroup rec.gambling.poker (rgp). We get together in different poker friendly locations throughout the US during the year to have fun and PLAY POKER. Our biggest annual meeting is called BARGE and it is held in Las Vegas in August. ATLARGE is the Atlantic City version, and is held in March of every year.

ATLARGE stands for: ATLantic city Annual RecGambling Excursion. Historically, the attendance at ATLARGE is second only to BARGE. Last year, over 180 players from all over the planet brought the good times to Atlantic City for four days of fun and poker. ATLARGE 2005 will be held at the fabulous TRUMP TAJ MAHAL, the largest card room in Atlantic City.

How Can You Participate in ATLARGE 2005?

EVERYONE IS WELCOME! But, because we all originally met over the Internet, the ONLY rule for participation in any of our events is that you MUST register over the Internet. Then, all you have to do is pay for the events you wish to play in, and show up and have fun. But, you must register and pay HERE. There will be no on-site registration for ATLARGE.

ATLARGE TRADITIONS

One ATLARGE tradition of which we're particularly proud is our generosity to the dealers. Of your $75 tournament buy-ins on Friday and Sunday, $10 is the house rake, and $5 goes to the dealer toke pool (this makes the dealer toke pool 8.33% of the entire prize pool, something we can ALL take pride in)

BUT, for our main event, the NO LIMIT HOLDEM tourney on Saturday, PokerStars is picking up BOTH the tokes and the rake. Thanks to their generosity, every penny of your buy-in will be paid in prizes to the winners, and the dealers are still treated with typical ATLARGE generosity.

TRANSPORTATION:

Unfortunately, for those planning to fly, ATLANTIC CITY is not terribly easy to reach. There is an airport just nine miles from Atlantic City, but regularly scheduled flights there are usually BRUTALLY EXPENSIVE. Once at the Atlantic City airport, cabs are readily available. The PHILADELPHIA airport is 50 miles from Atlantic City, with regular train service for only $8. Pick up the SEPTA R1 train directly in front of the Philadelphia airport terminals, and take the train to 30th Street Station in Philadelphia (three stops after leaving the airport). Change trains at 30th Street for NJ Transit for Atlantic City. NJ Transit will drop you off at the Atlantic City train station 9 blocks off the boardwalk. Your NJ Transit ticket includes no-wait bus service to all casinos when you exit the train. Schedules for these trains are located at: Airport to 30th Street: http://www.septa.com/schedule.cgi?route=r1&day=1 30th Street to A.C.: http://www.njtransit.com/pdf/rail/current/r0090.pdf You are well advised to check the train schedules in advance, lest you be destined to repeat ADB Jester's overnight stay in 30th Street station at ATLARGE III, missing the Smoker.
