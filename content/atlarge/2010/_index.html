ATLARGE 2010 was held March 11-14, 2010 at the Trump Taj Mahal in Atlantic City. The events were:

Friday, March 12, 11am TOC-Style (Stud, Omaha8, Hold'em)
Saturday, March 13, 10am No-Limit Hold'em
Sunday, March 14, 11am Stud/8

(NOTE: TOC-Style and Stud8 were limit tournaments.)

The buy-in for each event was $100+20, paid to the Taj the morning of each event or prepaid at the Taj beginning on Friday. All events were NO REBUY.

There was also a $20 ATLARGE registration fee. The ATLARGE rates for rooms at the Taj were $85.00 on Friday and Saturday, and $65.00 on other nights.

What is "ATLARGE"?

ATLARGE is an annual gathering in Atlantic City of poker players who originally "met" and/or still participate on the Internet newsgroup rec.gambling.poker (rgp). We get together in different poker friendly locations throughout the US during the year to have fun and PLAY POKER. Our biggest annual meeting is called BARGE and it is held in Las Vegas in August. ATLARGE is the Atlantic City version, and is held in March of every year.

ATLARGE stands for the ATLantic city Annual Rec.Gambling Excursion. Historically, attendance at ATLARGE is second only to BARGE.

Unofficial ATLARGE Events

A private ATLARGE banquet was planned for Saturday, March 14, 2010 at the TAJ.

The "SMOKER" DINNER

The SMOKER DINNER was held at the OLD WATERWAY INN on Thursday night, March 11, 2010 at 8:00pm. Smoking was allowed, so a special "up wind" end of the table was reserved for non-smokers. The food was very good, the cigars were excellent, and the company was the best!

This meal was 'Dutch treat'. Everyone was expected to pay for what they eat and drink, plus a 20% gratuity for the wait staff.

Ring Games

The TAJ spread cash games at all limits, including an ARG favorite, the $7.50-$15.00 "pink chip" holdem game! Baby PL was also available.

Transportation

The PHILADELPHIA airport is 50 miles from Atlantic City, with regular train service for only $8. Pick up the SEPTA R1 train directly in front of the Philadelphia airport terminals, take it to 30th Street Station in Philadelphia, then change to NJ Transit for Atlantic City. Your NJ Transit ticket includes no-wait bus service to all casinos when you exit the train.
